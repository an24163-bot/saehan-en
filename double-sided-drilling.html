APPLICATION — Semiconductor Parts Requiring Precision Hole Drilling

Which Parts Require Both-Side Micro Hole Drilling?

Silicon electrodes, shower heads, and electrostatic chucks all share a common challenge: hundreds to thousands of precision through-holes drilled from both faces. The ANT-BSSD500 was designed specifically around these three parts and their hole drilling requirements.

Silicon Electrode

Silicon Electrode (Si)

The primary plasma-generating electrode in dry etch chambers. Both faces require hundreds to thousands of precision through-holes — our simultaneous both-side drilling eliminates the concentricity defects inherent in conventional single-direction machining.

Core Application

Shower Head

Shower Head (Si / SiC)

Gas distribution plate at the heart of CVD and ALD process chambers. Uniform hole position across the full plate is critical for gas distribution uniformity — our horizontal structure minimizes drill deflection for consistent hole-to-hole position accuracy.

Core Application

Wafer Chuck / ESC

Electrostatic Chuck (AlN)

Electrostatic chuck with thousands of micro vacuum holes across the chuck surface. AlN ceramic is extremely hard and brittle — our peck drilling cycle (0.2mm step-retract) prevents micro-crack propagation and maintains hole geometry throughout.

DIFFERENTIATION — Why ANT-BSSD500 Outperforms Conventional Drilling

Two Design Innovations That Solve Real Production Problems

Every feature of the ANT-BSSD500 was designed around a specific failure mode in conventional silicon electrode drilling. Here's what changed — and why it matters to your yield.

01 · Both-Side Simultaneous Drilling — The Root Cause of Concentricity Defects, Eliminated

BEFORE — Single-Direction (Conventional)

Machine one face → flip and re-fixture → machine second face: two-step process with mandatory repositioning

Re-fixturing introduces Pitch Error and concentricity defects — the dominant source of silicon electrode yield loss

Operator intervention required between faces → higher labor cost and longer cycle time

Alignment stack-up error compounds with every hole — yield decreases as hole count increases

AFTER — Both-Side Simultaneous (ANT-BSSD500)

Both spindles drill simultaneously — zero repositioning, zero re-fixturing error

Concentricity maintained at 20㎛ or better across 100% of holes

Single uninterrupted machining cycle — extended unattended run time, reduced labor cost

Hole quality remains stable even when machine pitch error occurs — robust, high-yield production

02 · Horizontal Machining Structure — Deep Holes That Vertical Machines Simply Cannot Drill

BEFORE — Vertical Type (Conventional)

Gravity pulls chips back into the hole — chip packing damages tools and worsens hole quality

Chip packing reduces coolant reach → thermal load increases → drill breakage risk rises

L/D ratio severely limited — deep hole drilling in silicon and SiC is not feasible

AFTER — Horizontal Type (ANT-BSSD500)

Horizontal axis aligns with gravity-assisted chip evacuation — chips exit the hole naturally

Clean chip evacuation keeps coolant path open → consistent thermal control → drill life extended

Same conditions: 1.5~2× throughput vs vertical; with optimized recipe: 2~3× throughput

Aspect ratio (L/D) up to 30 validated — enabling deep-hole electrode designs not possible before
