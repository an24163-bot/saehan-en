WIRE SAWING · ANT-MWS SERIES

Multi Wire Sawing Machine

Since the early 2000s, Saehan Nanotech has independently developed wire sawing machines for hard and brittle materials through dedicated in-house R&D. The ANT-MWS Series is the result of that accumulated expertise: a customizable multi-wire diamond wire sawing platform that delivers world-class productivity on the full spectrum of advanced materials — from semiconductor silicon ingots to SiC power device substrates and rare-earth permanent magnets.

APPLICATION AREAS

Industries We Serve

The ANT-MWS Series is deployed across multiple advanced industries where precision slicing of hard brittle materials is critical to production quality and cost.

⚡

Semiconductor & Display

Silicon ingot slicing for DRAM, NAND, and logic wafers. SiC substrate slicing for power devices. Sapphire substrate slicing for LED and compound semiconductor epi layers. Quartz and optical glass slicing for precision optics.

Si · SiC · Sapphire · Quartz · ALN
🚗

Electric Vehicle (EV) Motors

NdFeB rare-earth permanent magnets power over 90% of EV traction motors. Sintered NdFeB blocks must be precision-sliced into arc segments and thin wafers — a process that demands diamond wire sawing due to brittleness and metallic dust fire risk. Saehan Nanotech completed Korea's first motor magnet cutting project in 2024.

NdFeB · SmCo · Ferrite
💨

Wind & Renewable Energy

Direct-drive wind turbine generators use large NdFeB magnet arrays. Precision slicing of magnet blocks into arc segments with tight dimensional tolerances is essential to generator efficiency. Our machines handle ingot-scale rare-earth cutting for the energy transition supply chain.

NdFeB · SmCo · Rare Earth Alloys
🔬

Advanced Ceramics & Specialty Materials

Technical ceramics (alumina, ALN, SiC), graphite electrodes, artificial gemstones, and optical glass all require wire sawing for precision thickness control. Our recipe library covers the full range — from Mohs 9+ materials down to softer specialty glasses.

Ceramics · Graphite · Optical Glass · Crystal · Germanium
